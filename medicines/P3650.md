#EN
### 💊 덱펜정(덱시부프로펜) - Indications and Usage
* This medication is used as an adjunctive treatment for chronic polyarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, painful edema or inflammation following trauma and surgery, and infections accompanied by inflammation, pain, and fever.

### 🕒 덱펜정(덱시부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
* For adults, take 1 tablet (300 mg) per dose, 2 to 4 times a day. Do not exceed 4 tablets (1,200 mg) per day.

### ⚠️ 덱펜정(덱시부프로펜) - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Taking this medication increases the risk of serious, potentially fatal cardiovascular thrombotic reactions, myocardial infarction, and stroke, and may cause severe gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation.
* Do not take this medication if you have a hypersensitivity to it, have or have experienced gastrointestinal ulcers, gastrointestinal, cerebrovascular, or other bleeding, severe blood disorders, severe liver dysfunction, severe renal dysfunction, severe cardiac insufficiency, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma or a history of it, a history of asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), are undergoing pain treatment before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, are pregnant for 6 months or more, are breastfeeding, have inflammatory bowel disease such as Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis, or have a history of gastrointestinal bleeding or perforation due to previous NSAID treatment.

Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have or have experienced blood disorders, a bleeding tendency, liver dysfunction or a history of it, renal dysfunction or a history of it, fluid retention or heart failure, hypertension, a history of hypersensitivity, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) and mixed connective tissue disease (MCTD), are elderly, are a child aged 6 years or older, have stomach cancer, are an alcoholic, are pregnant in the first or second trimester or are a woman who may be pregnant, have ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, cerebrovascular disease, or risk factors for cardiovascular disease (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking), hepatic porphyria, have undergone major surgery resulting in blood loss, or have blood coagulation disorders or are receiving anticoagulant therapy.

Considering the importance of drug administration, either discontinue breastfeeding or discontinue drug administration.

Dizziness or fatigue may occur after taking this medication, so exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.

For acute conditions, long-term administration should generally be limited to within 5 days.
