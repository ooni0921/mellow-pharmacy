### 💊 오스틴이부프로펜정400밀리그램 - Efficacy and Effects
*   This medicine is used for rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), fever and pain due to colds, back pain, dysmenorrhea, post-operative pain, ankylosing spondylitis, headache, toothache, muscle pain, neuralgia, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injuries (sprains, contusions), non-articular rheumatic diseases (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis), and fever in acute upper respiratory tract inflammation in children.

### 🕒 오스틴이부프로펜정400밀리그램 - Dosage and Administration
*   For rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, soft tissue injuries, non-articular rheumatic diseases, acute gout, and psoriatic arthritis, adults take 1/2 to 1.5 tablets (200-600 mg) 3 to 4 times a day, with a maximum daily dose of 3200 mg.

For juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, the dose is 30-40 mg per kg of body weight daily, divided into 3 to 4 doses. For mild to moderate pain or colds, adults take 1/2 to 1 tablet (200-400 mg) 3 to 4 times a day.

Children aged 11-14 years take 200-250 mg once, 7-10 years take 150-200 mg once, 3-6 years take 100-150 mg once, and 1-2 years take 50-100 mg once, orally 3 to 4 times a day.

Children weighing less than 30 kg should not exceed a daily dose of 500 mg, and it is advisable to avoid taking the medicine on an empty stomach.

Do not exceed the recommended dose.

For suppositories, children should have 3-6 mg per kg of body weight inserted deeply into the rectum twice daily.

The dose may be appropriately increased or decreased depending on age and symptoms.

### ⚠️ 오스틴이부프로펜정400밀리그램 - Precautions
*   If you are a regular drinker consuming three or more alcoholic beverages daily, consult a doctor or pharmacist when taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Taking this medicine may increase the risk of serious cardiovascular thrombotic reactions, myocardial infarction, and stroke, which can be fatal. Serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation may also occur.

Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking) who require high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day) should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to this medicine, gastrointestinal ulcer, a history of gastrointestinal bleeding or ulcer, severe blood abnormalities, severe liver impairment, severe kidney impairment, severe cardiac dysfunction, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma or a history thereof, or a history of hypersensitivity reactions such as asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other NSAIDs (including COX-2 inhibitors), if you are experiencing pain before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, if you are a pregnant woman in the last trimester, if you are receiving high-dose methotrexate as chemotherapy, or if you have genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have or have a history of blood disorders, a bleeding tendency, liver cirrhosis, liver impairment, kidney impairment, cardiac dysfunction or heart disease, hypertension, hypersensitivity reactions, systemic lupus erythematosus and mixed connective tissue disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, if you are elderly, a child, pregnant, or breastfeeding.

Do not exceed the recommended dose of this medicine.
