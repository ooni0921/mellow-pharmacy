##EN
### 💊 Mag-N-New Soft Capsule - Indications and Effects
*   This medicine is used for the supply of vitamins B1, B2, B6, and E during physical fatigue, pregnancy and lactation, periods of physical decline during illness or convalescence (during or after recovery from illness), and in old age.
*   It provides relief for shoulder and neck stiffness, numbness in hands and feet, and cold hands and feet due to peripheral circulatory disorders and during menopause.
*   It is used for beriberi, eye fatigue, neuralgia, myalgia, arthralgia (such as low back pain, shoulder stiffness).
*   It aids in the relief of angular stomatitis (inflammation at the corners of the mouth), cheilitis (inflammation of the lips), stomatitis (inflammation in the mouth), glossitis (inflammation of the tongue), eczema, and dermatitis.
*   It is also used for muscle cramps due to magnesium deficiency.

### 🕒 Mag-N-New Soft Capsule - Dosage and Administration
*   Adults and children over 12 years of age should take 1 capsule once, twice a day.

### ⚠️ Mag-N-New Soft Capsule - Precautions
*   No information available
*   Do not take this medicine if you are hypersensitive to this drug, hypersensitive to soy or peanuts, have experienced an allergic reaction to soybean oil, have severe renal failure, or are an infant under 12 months of age.
*   Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are pregnant or possibly pregnant, breastfeeding, a premature infant, an infant, have cardiac and circulatory system dysfunction, renal impairment, hypoproteinemia, hyperlipoproteinemia, lipid metabolism disorders such as diabetic hyperlipidemia and pancreatitis, patients requiring careful administration of lipid emulsions, or patients at special risk of fat overload.
*   Adhere strictly to the prescribed dosage and administration.
*   This medicine may turn urine yellow and affect clinical test results.
*   If symptoms do not improve after approximately 1 month of administration, discontinue use immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
