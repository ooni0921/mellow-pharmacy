#EN

### 💊 덱스프리연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Efficacy and Effects
*   This medicine is used for chronic polyarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis.
*   It is also used as an adjunctive treatment for painful swelling or inflammation after trauma and surgery, inflammation and pain, and infections accompanied by fever.

### 🕒 덱스프리연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
*   Adults should take 1 tablet at a time, 2 to 4 times a day.
*   Do not exceed 4 tablets (1,200 mg) per day.

### ⚠️ 덱스프리연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Precautions
*   Consult your doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and are taking this medicine or other fever reducers/pain relievers, as it may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.
*   Taking this medicine may increase the risk of serious, potentially fatal cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke, as well as severe gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation.
*   Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose dexibuprofen (1,200 mg per day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking) who require high-dose dexibuprofen should consult their doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to it, have or have a history of bronchial asthma, or have experienced asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
*   Do not take this medicine if you have or show signs of a gastrointestinal ulcer, have a history of recurrent ulcers, have gastrointestinal, cerebrovascular, or other bleeding, have inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis), or have a history of gastrointestinal bleeding or perforation due to previous NSAID use.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have severe blood disorders, liver impairment, kidney impairment, heart failure, or hypertension.
*   Do not take this medicine if you are undergoing pain treatment before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, or if you are pregnant for 6 months or more.
*   Consult your doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine if you have or have a history of blood disorders, liver impairment, kidney impairment; have bleeding disorders or a tendency to bleed; have fluid retention or heart failure, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus, or mixed connective tissue disease; have stomach cancer, alcoholism, ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, cerebrovascular disease, hepatic porphyria; have cardiovascular risk factors such as hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, or smoking; or have undergone major surgery resulting in blood loss.
*   Consult your doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine if you are in early or mid-pregnancy, after 20 weeks of pregnancy, planning pregnancy, are potentially pregnant, are elderly, are a child aged 6 years or older, have experienced hypersensitivity reactions, or have or have a history of hypersensitivity to Yellow No. 5 (Sunset Yellow FCF).
*   If you are breastfeeding, discontinue this medicine or discontinue breastfeeding.
*   Limit long-term administration to within 5 days.
*   Dizziness or fatigue may occur. Exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.
