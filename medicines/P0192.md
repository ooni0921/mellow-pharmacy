# EN

### 💊 이부펜정400mg(이부프로펜) - Indications and Effects
* This medication is used for rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), fever and pain due to the common cold, low back pain, dysmenorrhea, postoperative pain, ankylosing spondylitis, headache, toothache, muscle pain, neuralgia, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injury (sprains, contusions), and non-articular rheumatic diseases (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis).

### 🕒 이부펜정400mg(이부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
* For rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, soft tissue injury, non-articular rheumatic diseases, acute gout, and psoriatic arthritis, adults should take 1/2 to 1.5 tablets (200-600 mg) per dose, 3-4 times daily. The maximum daily dose is 3200 mg.

For juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, children should take 30-40 mg per kg of body weight daily, divided into 3-4 doses. For mild to moderate pain or the common cold, adults should take 1/2 to 1 tablet (200-400 mg) per dose, 3-4 times daily.

Children aged 11-14 years should take 200-250 mg per dose; 7-10 years, 150-200 mg per dose; 3-6 years, 100-150 mg per dose; and 1-2 years, 50-100 mg per dose, 3-4 times daily orally.

Children weighing less than 30 kg should not exceed a daily dose of 500 mg, and it is advisable to avoid taking this medication on an empty stomach.

Do not exceed the recommended dose.

### ⚠️ 이부펜정400mg(이부프로펜) - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are a regular drinker (three or more drinks daily) and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics, as it may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Taking this medication increases the risk of serious cardiovascular thrombotic reactions, myocardial infarction, and stroke, which can be fatal. Serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation may also occur.

Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking) who require high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day) should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medication.
* Do not take this medication if you have hypersensitivity to it, a history of gastrointestinal ulcer or bleeding, severe blood disorders, severe liver impairment, severe kidney impairment, severe heart failure, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma or a history thereof, a history of hypersensitivity reactions such as asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (including COX-2 inhibitors), if you are experiencing pain before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, if you are in the last trimester (last 3 months) of pregnancy, if you are undergoing high-dose methotrexate chemotherapy, or if you have hereditary problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have a history of blood disorders or bleeding tendencies, cirrhosis, liver impairment or a history thereof, kidney impairment or a history thereof, heart failure or heart disease, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus and mixed connective tissue disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, if you are elderly or a child, pregnant, breastfeeding, or if you have hypersensitivity to Sunset Yellow FCF (Yellow No. 5).
