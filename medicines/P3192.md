*   Regular drinkers who consume three or more alcoholic beverages daily should consult a doctor or pharmacist when taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics, as it may cause liver damage.

    Severe skin reactions such as Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis (AGEP), Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS), and Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN), which can be very rarely fatal, have been reported. If signs of a skin rash or other hypersensitivity reactions appear after taking this medicine, discontinue use immediately.

    Do not exceed the daily maximum dose (4,000 mg) of acetaminophen. It may cause liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.

    If severe skin adverse reactions such as Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis appear, discontinue use and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to it or a history of it, a history of asthma when taking other antipyretic analgesics or cold medicines, are an infant under 3 months old, or have severe or uncontrolled hypertension.

    Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are: a child under 2 years old; an infant or child under 15 years old infected with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza; experience symptoms of ischemic colitis (e.g., sudden abdominal pain, rectal bleeding) related to pseudoephedrine component; have an allergic constitution; have a history of drug-induced allergic symptoms (e.g., fever, rash, joint pain, asthma, itching); have liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, hypertension, gastro-duodenal ulcer, glaucoma (e.g., eye pain, blurred vision), difficulty in urination; are elderly; are frail or have a high fever; have persistent or recurrent gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, stomach discomfort, stomach pain, or have ulcer or bleeding problems; are pregnant or possibly pregnant; are breastfeeding; smoke; have asthma, chronic bronchitis, emphysema, a cough with excessive phlegm, a cough lasting more than 1 week or recurrent cough, chronic cough, or a cough accompanied by fever, rash, or persistent headache.
