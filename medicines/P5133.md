### 💊 도나펜에프연질캡슐 - Efficacy and Effects
* This medication is used for the relief of pain associated with headache, toothache, post-extraction pain, sore throat (pharyngodynia), earache, joint pain, neuralgia, lower back pain, muscle pain, shoulder stiffness/pain (견통), bruise pain, fracture pain, sprain pain (염좌통), menstrual pain (dysmenorrhea), and traumatic pain, as well as for antipyresis in cases of chills and fever.

### 🕒 도나펜에프연질캡슐 - Dosage and Administration
* For adults and individuals 15 years of age or older, take 1 capsule per dose, 1 to 3 times daily, avoiding an empty stomach.
* Maintain a dosing interval of at least 4 hours.

### ⚠️ 도나펜에프연질캡슐 - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are a regular drinker consuming three or more alcoholic beverages daily and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking) who require high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day) should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medication.
* Do not take this medication if you have hypersensitivity to this drug or its components, have experienced an asthmatic reaction when taking this drug, its components, other antipyretic analgesics, or cold medicines, are in the 30th week of pregnancy or later, or are an infant under 3 months of age.

Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if:
    * You are an infant (newborn) or child under 15 years old with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza infection.
    * You, your parents, or siblings have a predisposition to conditions such as hives, contact dermatitis, bronchial asthma, allergic rhinitis, migraines, or food allergies.
    * You have previously experienced allergic reactions to medication (e.g., fever, rash, joint pain, asthma, itching).
    * You have liver disease, kidney disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, or hypertension.
    * You are physically frail or have a high fever.
    * You are elderly.
    * You are pregnant or may be pregnant, or are breastfeeding.
    * You are currently undergoing medical treatment (e.g., taking medication for diabetes, gout, arthritis, anticoagulants, steroids, or other drugs).
    * You have persistent or recurrent gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, stomach discomfort, or stomach pain, or have a history of ulcers or bleeding.
    * You have lost significant fluids due to vomiting and diarrhea, are unable to absorb fluids, or are taking diuretics.
    * You are taking low-dose aspirin to prevent myocardial infarction or stroke.

If symptoms do not improve after 5 to 6 doses, discontinue use immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.

Strictly adhere to the prescribed dosage and administration.

Do not consume alcohol while taking this medication.
