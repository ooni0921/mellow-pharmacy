### 💊 OMEKOEFJEONG - Indications & Effects
*   This medicine is used to relieve various cold symptoms such as runny nose, nasal congestion, sneezing, sore throat, chills, fever, headache, joint pain, and muscle pain.

### 🕒 OMEKOEFJEONG - Dosage & Administration
*   For individuals aged 15 years and older, and adults, take 1 tablet once, 3 times daily. For individuals aged 11 years or older but under 15 years, take 1 tablet once, 2 times daily, or 2 tablets once, 1 time daily. All doses should be taken 30 minutes after meals.

### ⚠️ OMEKOEFJEONG - Precautions
*   Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and are taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics, as liver damage may occur.

Very rarely, severe skin reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis (AGEP), Stevens-Johnson syndrome (SJS), and toxic epidermal necrolysis (TEN, Lyell's syndrome), which can be fatal, have been reported. If skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reactions appear after taking this medicine, discontinue use immediately.

Do not exceed the maximum daily dose (4,000 mg) of acetaminophen. Liver damage may occur. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.

If severe skin adverse reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis appear, discontinue use and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have a history of hypersensitivity to this medicine or have experienced it before, have experienced asthma when taking this medicine, other antipyretic analgesics, or cold medicines, are an infant under 3 months of age or a child under 2 years of age, are in the 30th week of pregnancy or later, or have hereditary problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if it is absolutely necessary for children under 2 years of age, infants and children under 15 years of age who are infected with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza, pregnant women or women who may be pregnant, breastfeeding women, individuals with allergic predispositions, the elderly, those with weakened bodies or high fever.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have experienced symptoms of ischemic colitis such as sudden abdominal pain or rectal bleeding related to pseudoephedrine, have experienced allergic symptoms such as fever, rash, joint pain, asthma, or itching due to medicine, have liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, hypertension, gastric/duodenal ulcer, glaucoma, or difficulty urinating, or if gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, gastric discomfort, or stomach pain persist or recur, or if you have ulcer or bleeding problems, or if you smoke, have asthma, chronic bronchitis, emphysema, a cough accompanied by excessive phlegm, a cough that persists for more than 1 week or recurs, chronic cough, or a cough accompanied by fever, rash, or persistent headache.

If symptoms do not improve after 5 to 6 doses, discontinue this medicine immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.

Children should take this medicine under the guidance and supervision of a guardian.

Do not continue taking for a long period.

Drowsiness may occur during administration. Avoid driving a car or operating machinery.

Strictly adhere to the prescribed dosage and administration.
