#EN
### 💊 디펜스코연질캡슐 - Efficacy and Effects
* This medication is used to relieve various symptoms of the common cold (runny nose, nasal congestion, sneezing, sore throat, cough, phlegm, chills, fever, headache, joint pain, muscle pain).

### 🕒 디펜스코연질캡슐 - Dosage and Administration
* For individuals aged 15 years and older, take 2 capsules once, 3 times a day. For individuals aged 8 years and older but under 15 years, take 1 capsule once, 3 times a day, 30 minutes after meals.

### ⚠️ 디펜스코연질캡슐 - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics, as it may cause liver damage.

* Severe skin reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, and toxic epidermal necrolysis (Lyell syndrome), which can be fatal in very rare cases, have been reported. If skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reaction appear after taking this medication, stop taking it immediately.

* Do not exceed the maximum daily dose (4,000 mg) of acetaminophen. It may cause liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.

* If severe skin adverse reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis occur, stop taking the medication and consult a doctor or pharmacist.

* Do not take this medication if you have a hypersensitivity to it or have experienced it before, if you have experienced asthma when taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics or cold medicines, if you are an infant under 3 months of age, or if you have severe or uncontrolled hypertension.

* Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are an infant or child under 15 years old who is infected with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza; if you develop symptoms of ischemic colitis (e.g., sudden abdominal pain, rectal bleeding) related to pseudoephedrine; if you have an allergic constitution, or have experienced allergic reactions to medication (e.g., fever, rash, joint pain, asthma, itching); if you have liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, hypertension, gastric or duodenal ulcer, glaucoma (e.g., eye pain, blurred vision), or difficulty urinating; if you are elderly, frail, or have a high fever; if you have persistent or recurrent gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, stomach discomfort, or stomach pain, or have ulcer or bleeding issues; or if you are pregnant, possibly pregnant, breastfeeding, or a child under 2 years old.

* Before taking this medication, patients with a cough (cough associated with smoking, asthma, chronic bronchitis, emphysema, excessive phlegm; cough lasting or recurring for more than 1 week; chronic cough; cough accompanied by fever, rash, or persistent headache) should consult a doctor or pharmacist.

* If symptoms do not improve after 5 to 6 doses, stop taking the medication immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.

* Adhere strictly to the prescribed dosage and administration.

* When used in children, administer under the guidance and supervision of a guardian.

* Drowsiness may occur after taking this medication, so exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.
