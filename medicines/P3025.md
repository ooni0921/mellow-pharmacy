### 💊 Hanpoong Nose-E
*   This medicine is used to relieve various symptoms of the common cold (runny nose, nasal congestion, sneezing, sore throat, chills, fever, headache, joint pain, muscle pain).

### 🕒 Hanpoong Nose-E - Dosage and Administration
*   Adults aged 15 years or older should take 1 pouch (90 mL) at a time. Children aged 11 to under 15 years should take 2/3 pouch (approx. 60 mL) at a time. Children aged 7 to under 11 years should take 1/2 pouch (approx. 45 mL) at a time. Children aged 3 to under 7 years should take 1/3 pouch (approx. 30 mL) at a time. Children aged 2 to under 3 years should take 1/4 pouch (approx. 22.5 mL) at a time. Take 3 times a day, 30 minutes after meals. If necessary, it can be taken at bedtime, and generally, the dosing interval should be 4 hours.

### ⚠️ Hanpoong Nose-E - Precautions
*   Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily when taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause liver damage.

Severe skin reactions such as Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis (AGEP), Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS), and Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN), which can be fatal, have been reported very rarely. If skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reactions appear after taking this medicine, discontinue use immediately.

Do not exceed the maximum daily dose of acetaminophen (4,000 mg). It may cause liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have a history of hypersensitivity to this medicine or its components, or to other antipyretic analgesics or cold medicines that caused asthma, or if you are an infant under 3 months of age or a child under 2 years of age.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are an infant or child under 15 years of age infected with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza; a child under 2 years of age (only if absolutely necessary); a person with a predisposition to hives, contact dermatitis, bronchial asthma, allergic rhinitis, migraine, food allergies, etc.; a patient who has previously experienced allergic symptoms to medication; a person with liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, high blood pressure, edema, gastroduodenal ulcer, glaucoma, dysuria; an elderly person; a physically weak person or a person with high fever; a person with persistent or recurring gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, stomach discomfort, stomach pain, or with ulcer or bleeding problems; a pregnant woman or a woman who may be pregnant; a breastfeeding woman; a person who smokes; a person with asthma, chronic bronchitis, emphysema, a cough accompanied by excessive phlegm, a cough lasting or recurring for more than one week, chronic cough, or a cough accompanied by fever, rash, or persistent headache.

Adhere strictly to the prescribed dosage and administration.

Do not take continuously for a long period.

When administering to children, do so under the guidance and supervision of a guardian.

Drowsiness may occur after taking this medicine, so be cautious when driving or operating machinery.

If symptoms do not improve after 5 to 6 doses, discontinue use immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
