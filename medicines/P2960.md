# EN
### 💊 코리투살에프시럽(이부프로펜) - Efficacy and Effects
*   This medication is used for rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), fever and pain due to colds, low back pain, dysmenorrhea, post-surgical pain, ankylosing spondylitis, headache, toothache, muscle pain, neuralgia, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injury (sprains, contusions), and non-articular rheumatic diseases (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis).

### 🕒 코리투살에프시럽(이부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
*   For ages 11-14: 200-250 mg (10-13 mL) once, 3-4 times a day.
*   For ages 7-10: 150-200 mg (8-10 mL) once, 3-4 times a day.
*   For ages 3-6: 100-150 mg (5-8 mL) once, 3-4 times a day.
*   For ages 1-2: 50-100 mg (3-5 mL) once, 3-4 times a day.

Children weighing less than 30 kg should not exceed a daily dose of 500 mg (25 mL), and it is advisable to avoid administration on an empty stomach.

### ⚠️ 코리투살에프시럽(이부프로펜) - Precautions
*   Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily and are taking this medication or other fever reducers/pain relievers. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

This medication may increase the risk of serious cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke, which can be fatal. It may also cause severe gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as bleeding, ulceration, and perforation.

Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high doses of ibuprofen (2,400 mg per day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking) who require high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg per day) should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medication.
*   Do not take this medication if you have a hypersensitivity to this drug, gastrointestinal ulcers, gastrointestinal bleeding, severe blood disorders, severe liver impairment, severe kidney impairment, severe cardiac dysfunction, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma, asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions caused by aspirin or other NSAIDs, pain before or after coronary artery bypass graft surgery, are in the last trimester of pregnancy, are breastfeeding, are receiving high-dose methotrexate for chemotherapy, or have genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

Do not exceed the recommended dose.

Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have blood disorders, a bleeding tendency, liver cirrhosis, liver impairment, kidney impairment, cardiac dysfunction, heart disease, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus, mixed connective tissue disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, are elderly, are under 2 years of age, are pregnant, or have chickenpox.

For cold symptoms, generally take for no more than 5 days. Do not take for more than 10 days for pain (adults) or 5 days for pain (children), or more than 3 days for fever, without the direction of a doctor or pharmacist.
