#EN
### 💊 다펜덱시연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Efficacy and Effects
*   This medicine is used for chronic polyarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis.
*   This medicine is used as an adjunctive treatment for painful edema or inflammation following trauma and surgery, and for infections accompanied by inflammation, pain, and fever.

### 🕒 다펜덱시연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
*   Adults should take 1 tablet at a time, 2 to 4 times a day.
*   Do not exceed 4 tablets per day.

### ⚠️ 다펜덱시연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Precautions
*   If you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily, consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.
*   Taking this medicine may increase the risk of serious cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke, which can be fatal. Serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation may also occur.
*   Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose dexibuprofen (1,200 mg/day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors such as hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, and smoking should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine if high-dose ibuprofen is required.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to this medicine, if you have or have experienced bronchial asthma, or if you have experienced asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
*   Do not take this medicine if you have or show signs of gastrointestinal ulcers, or a history of recurrence; if you have bleeding in the gastrointestinal tract, cerebrovascular, or other areas; if you have inflammatory bowel disease such as Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis; or if you have a history of gastrointestinal bleeding or perforation due to previous NSAIDs.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have severe blood disorders, liver dysfunction, kidney dysfunction, heart failure, hypertension, if you are undergoing pain treatment before or after coronary artery bypass grafting, or if you are pregnant for 6 months or more.
*   Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have or have experienced blood disorders, liver dysfunction, kidney dysfunction; if you have blood coagulation disorders or bleeding tendencies; if you have fluid retention, heart failure, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus, or mixed connective tissue disease; if you have stomach cancer, alcoholism, ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, cerebrovascular disease, or hepatic porphyria; if you have cardiovascular risk factors such as hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, or smoking; or if you have undergone major surgery resulting in blood loss.
*   Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are in early or mid-pregnancy, after 20 weeks of pregnancy, planning a pregnancy, or potentially pregnant; if you are elderly; if you are a child over 6 years old; if you have experienced hypersensitivity reactions; or if you have gastrointestinal ulcers due to long-term NSAID use in the past and require long-term administration of this medicine, while concurrently receiving treatment for peptic ulcers with misoprostol or similar drugs.
*   If you are breastfeeding, discontinue this medicine or stop breastfeeding.
*   Long-term administration should be within 5 days.
*   Dizziness or fatigue may occur. Exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.
