#EN
### 💊 큐센400이부프로펜연질캡슐 - Efficacy and Effects
* This medicine is used for fever and pain due to common cold, lower back pain, menstrual pain, rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), postoperative pain, headache, migraine, toothache, muscle pain, neuralgia, ankylosing spondylitis, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injuries (sprains, contusions), and non-articular rheumatic diseases (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis).

### 🕒 큐센400이부프로펜연질캡슐 - Dosage and Administration
* For rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, soft tissue injuries, non-articular rheumatic diseases, acute gout, and psoriatic arthritis, adults take 1/2 to 1.5 capsules (200-600 mg) once, 3 to 4 times a day, up to a maximum of 8 capsules (3,200 mg) per day.

For juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, take 30-40 mg per kg of body weight per day, divided into 3 to 4 doses. For mild to moderate pain or common cold, adults take 1/2 to 1 capsule (200-400 mg) once, 3 to 4 times a day.

For migraine, adults take 1/2 to 1 capsule (200-400 mg) once, and do not exceed 1 capsule (400 mg) within 24 hours.

Do not exceed the recommended dose.

Adjust appropriately according to age and symptoms.

### ⚠️ 큐센400이부프로펜연질캡슐 - Precautions
* Regular drinkers who consume three or more alcoholic beverages daily should consult a doctor or pharmacist when taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Taking this medicine increases the risk of serious, potentially fatal cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke, and may cause serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulcers, and perforation.
* Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to this medicine, gastrointestinal ulcers and gastrointestinal bleeding or a history thereof, severe blood disorders, severe liver dysfunction, severe kidney dysfunction, severe heart failure, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma or a history thereof, hypersensitivity such as asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions caused by aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), patients experiencing pain before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, or pregnant women in the last trimester (final 3 months) of pregnancy.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have a history of hypersensitivity, blood disorders, bleeding tendency, liver cirrhosis and liver dysfunction or a history thereof, kidney dysfunction or a history thereof, heart failure or heart disease, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus and mixed connective tissue disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, elderly patients and children, pregnant women, breastfeeding women, or a history of hypersensitivity or allergic reaction to Sunset Yellow FCF (Yellow No. 5).

Do not exceed the recommended dose.

When taking for a common cold, generally take for no more than 5 days. Do not take for pain for more than 10 days (adults) or 5 days (children) without a doctor's or pharmacist's instruction, and do not take for fever for more than 3 days.
