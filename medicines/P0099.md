#EN
### 💊 스코펜정400밀리그램(이부프로펜) - Efficacy and Effects
* This drug is used for rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), fever and pain due to common cold, lower back pain, dysmenorrhea, post-operative pain, ankylosing spondylitis, headache, toothache, myalgia, neuralgia, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injuries (sprains, contusions), and non-articular rheumatic diseases (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis).

### 🕒 스코펜정400밀리그램(이부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
* For rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, soft tissue injuries, non-articular rheumatic diseases, acute gout, and psoriatic arthritis, adults take 1/2 to 1.5 tablets (200-600 mg) per dose, 3-4 times daily. The maximum daily dose is 3200 mg.

For juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, the dose is 30-40 mg per kg of body weight daily, divided into 3-4 doses. For mild to moderate pain or common cold, adults take 1/2 to 1 tablet (200-400 mg) per dose, 3-4 times daily.

Children aged 11-14 years take 200-250 mg per dose, 7-10 years take 150-200 mg per dose, 3-6 years take 100-150 mg per dose, and 1-2 years take 50-100 mg per dose, 3-4 times daily orally.

Children weighing less than 30 kg should not exceed a daily dose of 500 mg, and it is advisable to avoid taking the drug on an empty stomach.

Do not exceed the recommended dose.

### ⚠️ 스코펜정400밀리그램(이부프로펜) - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily and are taking this or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Taking this drug may increase the risk of serious cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke, which can be fatal. Serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation may also occur.

Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking) who require high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg/day) should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this drug.
* Do not take this drug if you have a hypersensitivity to it, have a history of or current gastrointestinal ulceration or bleeding, severe blood disorders, severe hepatic impairment, severe renal impairment, severe cardiac dysfunction, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma or a history thereof, hypersensitivity reactions such as asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (including COX-2 inhibitors), pain occurring before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, are in the last trimester of pregnancy (last 3 months), are taking high-dose methotrexate for chemotherapy, or have genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

Before taking this drug, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have a history of or current blood disorders, bleeding tendencies, liver cirrhosis, hepatic impairment or a history thereof, renal impairment or a history thereof, cardiac dysfunction or heart disease, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus, mixed connective tissue disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, are elderly or a child, are pregnant, lactating, or have hypersensitivity to Sunset Yellow FCF.
