###EN
### 💊 Benfobel S Tab - Efficacy and Effects
*   This medicine is used for the supplementation of vitamins D, B1, B2, B6 in cases of physical fatigue, during pregnancy and lactation, physical debility during or after illness (during illness or after recovery), during growth periods, and in old age. It is also used for the poor development of bones and teeth, prevention of rickets, beriberi, eye strain, neuralgia, myalgia, arthralgia (e.g., lower back pain, stiff shoulders), alleviation of symptoms such as angular cheilitis (inflammation at the corners of the mouth), cheilitis (inflammation of the lips), stomatitis (inflammation of the mouth), glossitis (inflammation of the tongue), eczema, dermatitis, and for the supplementation of zinc.

### 🕒 Benfobel S Tab - Dosage and Administration
*   Adults aged 19 years and older should take 1 tablet once daily.

### ⚠️ Benfobel S Tab - Precautions
*   No information
*   Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to this medicine, hypercalcemia (a condition where there is an excessive amount of calcium in the blood), sarcoidosis (a systemic inflammatory disease of unknown cause), kidney disease, are an infant under 12 months of age, have kidney stones, or have severe renal failure.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are pregnant, possibly pregnant, or breastfeeding; if you are a premature infant or young child; if you have impaired cardiac or circulatory function, renal impairment, hypoproteinemia, a history of kidney stones; if you have lipid metabolism disorders such as hyperlipoproteinemia, diabetic hyperlipidemia, pancreatitis, or if you require careful administration of lipid emulsions; if you are elderly; or if you have folic acid deficiency.

Adhere strictly to the prescribed dosage and administration.

This medicine may turn urine yellow and affect clinical test results.

If symptoms do not improve after approximately 1 month of administration, discontinue the medicine immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
