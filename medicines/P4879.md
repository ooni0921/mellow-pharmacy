### π’ νμΈνμ•„μ„ΈνΈνμ‹λ½(μ•„μ„ΈνΈμ•„λ―Έλ…Έν) - Indications & Efficacy
* This medicine is used for fever and pain due to colds, headache, neuralgia, myalgia (muscle pain), menstrual pain, sprain pain, toothache, arthralgia (joint pain), and rheumatic pain.

### π•’ νμΈνμ•„μ„ΈνΈνμ‹λ½(μ•„μ„ΈνΈμ•„λ―Έλ…Έν) - Dosage & Administration
* Children aged 12 years or younger should take the recommended single dose every 4 to 6 hours as needed.

This medicine should be taken at the lowest effective dose for the shortest possible duration, and should not exceed 5 doses per day (75 mg/kg).

Refer to the approved product information for recommended dosages by age group.

### β οΈ νμΈνμ•„μ„ΈνΈνμ‹λ½(μ•„μ„ΈνΈμ•„λ―Έλ…Έν) - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily and are taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause liver damage.

Very rarely, severe skin reactions such as Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis (AGEP), Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS), and Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN), which can be fatal, have been reported. If skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reaction appear after taking this medicine, discontinue use immediately.

Do not exceed the maximum daily dose of acetaminophen (4,000 mg). It may cause liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.
* Patients with hypersensitivity to this medicine, peptic ulcer, severe blood disorders, severe liver impairment, severe kidney impairment, severe cardiac dysfunction, aspirin asthma (asthma exacerbation induced by NSAIDs) or history thereof, patients taking barbiturates, tricyclic antidepressants, or alcohol should not take this medicine.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are a child under 2 years of age, pregnant or breastfeeding, have liver impairment, kidney impairment, history of peptic ulcer, blood disorders, bleeding tendency, cardiac dysfunction, history of hypersensitivity, bronchial asthma, or are elderly.
