### 💊 Dearmi-jeong - Efficacy and Effects
* Take one tablet daily for 21 days in the order indicated, followed by a 7-day tablet-free interval.
It is advisable to take the tablet at the same time each day if possible.
Menstruation usually occurs during the 7-day tablet-free interval, generally starting 2-3 days after the last tablet was taken.
If you continue to take this medication, start a new pack of tablets after a 7-day tablet-free interval. Menstruation may not have finished before you start a new pack.
For other detailed information, please refer to the full prescribing information.

### ⚠️ Dearmi-jeong - Precautions
* Smoking increases the risk of serious cardiovascular events such as thrombosis, so women taking oral contraceptives should refrain from smoking. Smokers over 35 years of age should not take this medication.
* Do not take this medication if you have hypersensitivity to this drug, tumors (e.g., breast cancer, endometrial cancer, pituitary tumors), thrombophlebitis, thromboembolism, cerebrovascular or coronary artery disease, undiagnosed abnormal genital bleeding, severe hypertension, severe liver impairment, Dubin-Johnson syndrome, Rotor syndrome, jaundice, cholestasis, hepatitis, sickle cell anemia, severe diabetes mellitus, severe hyperlipidemia, hyperlipoproteinemia, porphyria, metabolic disorders, persistent pruritus during pregnancy, herpes gestationis, pemphigoid gestationis, otosclerosis, if you are pregnant or may be pregnant, or a nursing mother, galactorrhea, hyperprolactinemia, migraines with aura such as amaurosis fugax or scintillating scotoma, pancreatitis, headaches with focal neurological symptoms such as aura, Hepatitis C, congenital or acquired hypercoagulopathy, if you are a smoker over 35 years of age, or have genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

Before taking this medication, consult with your doctor or pharmacist if you are a smoker, have hypertension, obesity, are a woman over 40, have epilepsy, migraine, a history of arterial disease or varicose veins, systemic lupus erythematosus, a history of breast cancer or depression, cardiac or renal failure, atypical hyperproliferative mastopathy, a history of breast nodules, valvular heart disease, atrial fibrillation, a history of hyperlipidemia, gallbladder disease or gallstones, endometriosis, asthma or chloasma, peripheral circulatory disorders, or diabetes mellitus.

This medication does not prevent HIV infection (AIDS) or other sexually transmitted diseases.

If you have severe gastrointestinal disorders, absorption may be incomplete, so use an additional contraceptive method.

This medication is not applicable to pre-menarcheal women and post-menopausal women.

For other detailed information, please refer to the full prescribing information.
