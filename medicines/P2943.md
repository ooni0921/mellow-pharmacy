# EN
### 💊 Dexibuprofen 300mg Tablets - Indications & Efficacy
*   This medication is used as an adjunct in the treatment of chronic polyarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, painful edema or inflammation following trauma and surgery, and infections accompanied by inflammation, pain, and fever.

### 🕒 Dexibuprofen 300mg Tablets - Dosage & Administration
*   Adults should take 1 tablet (300 mg) per dose, 2 to 4 times daily. Do not exceed 4 tablets (1,200 mg) per day.

### ⚠️ Dexibuprofen 300mg Tablets - Precautions
*   Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are a regular drinker consuming three or more alcoholic beverages daily and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Taking this medication may increase the risk of serious, potentially fatal cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke. Serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation may also occur.
*   Do not take this medication if you have hypersensitivity to this drug, a history of gastrointestinal ulcers, bleeding in the gastrointestinal tract, cerebrovascular, or other sites, severe blood disorders, severe liver dysfunction, severe kidney dysfunction, severe cardiac insufficiency, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma or a history thereof, a history of asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), are a patient undergoing pain treatment before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, are pregnant beyond 6 months, are breastfeeding, have inflammatory bowel disease such as Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis, or have a history of gastrointestinal bleeding or perforation due to previous NSAID treatment.

Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have or have a history of blood disorders, a bleeding tendency, liver dysfunction, kidney dysfunction, fluid retention or heart failure, hypertension, a history of hypersensitivity, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) and mixed connective tissue disease (MCTD), are elderly, are a child over 6 years of age, have gastric cancer, are an alcoholic, are pregnant in the first or second trimester or are a woman who may be pregnant, have ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, cerebrovascular disease, risk factors for cardiovascular disease (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking), hepatic porphyria, have undergone major surgery resulting in blood loss, have blood coagulation disorders, or are receiving anticoagulant therapy.

Considering the importance of drug administration, either discontinue breastfeeding or discontinue drug administration.

Dizziness or fatigue may occur after taking this medication, so exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.

For acute conditions, long-term administration should generally not exceed 5 days.
