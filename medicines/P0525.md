#EN
### 💊 조아이부프로펜400밀리그람정(수출용) - Indications and Usage
*   This medication is used for rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), fever and pain due to colds, low back pain, dysmenorrhea, post-operative pain, ankylosing spondylitis, headache, toothache, muscle pain, neuralgia, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injuries (sprains, strains), and non-articular rheumatic diseases (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis).

### 🕒 조아이부프로펜400밀리그람정(수출용) - Dosage and Administration
*   For rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, soft tissue injuries, non-articular rheumatic diseases, acute gout, and psoriatic arthritis, take 200-600 mg per dose, 3-4 times a day. A maximum of 3200 mg can be taken per day.

    For juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, take 30-40 mg per kg of body weight per day, divided into 3-4 doses.

    For mild to moderate pain and colds, take 200-400 mg per dose, 3-4 times a day. Adjust the dosage appropriately according to age and symptoms.

    For children, take the recommended single dose 3-4 times a day. Refer to the approved product information for recommended dosages by age group for children.

### ⚠️ 조아이부프로펜400밀리그람정(수출용) - Precautions
*   Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

    When taking non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), including this medication, serious cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke, which can be fatal, as well as serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as bleeding, ulceration, and perforation of the gastrointestinal tract, may occur.
*   Do not take this medication if you have hypersensitivity to this drug, gastrointestinal ulcer, gastrointestinal bleeding (current or history), severe blood disorders, severe liver dysfunction, severe kidney dysfunction, severe cardiac dysfunction, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma (current or history), hypersensitivity reactions such as asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other NSAIDs (including COX-2 inhibitors) (current or history), patients experiencing pain before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, pregnant women in the last trimester (last 3 months), patients receiving high-dose methotrexate as chemotherapy, or patients with genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

    Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have blood disorders (current or history), a tendency to bleed, liver cirrhosis, liver dysfunction (current or history), kidney dysfunction (current or history), cardiac dysfunction, heart disease, hypertension, hypersensitivity, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE), mixed connective tissue disease (MCTD), ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, if you are elderly, a child, pregnant, or breastfeeding.

    Do not exceed the recommended dose.

    In case of overdose, seek prompt medical attention even if there are no obvious symptoms or signs.
