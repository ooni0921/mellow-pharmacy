### 💊 Moakol Soft Capsule - Indications and Effects
* This medicine is used for the relief of various symptoms of the common cold (runny nose, stuffy nose, sneezing, sore throat, cough, phlegm, chills, fever, headache, joint pain, muscle pain).

### 🕒 Moakol Soft Capsule - Dosage and Administration
* For individuals aged 15 years and older, and adults: 2 capsules per dose. For individuals aged 8 years and older but under 15 years: 1 capsule per dose. Take 3 times daily, 30 minutes after meals.

### ⚠️ Moakol Soft Capsule - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and are taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause liver damage.

Very rarely, serious skin reactions such as Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis (AGEP), Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS), and Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN), which can be fatal, have been reported. If skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reactions appear after taking this medicine, discontinue use immediately.

Do not exceed the daily maximum dose of acetaminophen (4,000 mg). It may cause liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.

If severe skin adverse reactions such as Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis occur, discontinue use and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
* Do not take this medicine if you have a history of hypersensitivity to this drug, have experienced asthma when taking other antipyretic analgesics or cold medicines, are an infant under 3 months of age, have severe or uncontrolled hypertension, or are in the 30th week of pregnancy or later.

Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are an infant or child under 15 years of age infected with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza; if symptoms of ischemic colitis (e.g., sudden abdominal pain, rectal bleeding) related to pseudoephedrine occur; if you have an allergic constitution or history of allergic symptoms (e.g., fever, rash, joint pain, asthma, itching); if you have liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, hypertension, gastroduodenal ulcer, glaucoma (e.g., eye pain, blurred vision), difficulty urinating; if you are elderly, frail, or have a high fever; if you have persistent or recurrent gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, stomach discomfort, stomach pain, or have a history of ulcers or bleeding problems; if you are pregnant or may be pregnant, or are breastfeeding; if you have a cough accompanied by smoking, asthma, chronic bronchitis, emphysema, excessive phlegm, a cough lasting more than 1 week or recurrent, chronic cough, or a cough accompanied by fever, rash, or persistent headache; or if you are an infant under 2 years of age.

Follow the prescribed dosage and administration.

When administering to children, do so under the guidance and supervision of a guardian.

Drowsiness may occur after taking this medicine, so be careful when driving or operating machinery.

If symptoms do not improve after 5-6 doses, discontinue use immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
