#EN
### 💊 리오루펜정400밀리그람(이부프로펜) - Efficacy and Effects
* This medicine is used for rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), fever and pain due to common cold, low back pain, dysmenorrhea, post-operative pain, ankylosing spondylitis, headache, toothache, myalgia, neuralgia, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injury (sprains, contusions), and non-articular rheumatic diseases (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis).

### 🕒 리오루펜정400밀리그람(이부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
* For rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, soft tissue injury, non-articular rheumatic diseases, acute gout, and psoriatic arthritis, adults should take 1/2 to 1.5 tablets (200-600 mg) 3 to 4 times a day. The maximum daily dose is 8 tablets (3200 mg).
For juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, take 30-40 mg per kg of body weight daily, divided into 3 to 4 doses. For mild to moderate pain or common cold, adults should take 1/2 to 1 tablet (200-400 mg) 3 to 4 times a day.
Do not exceed the recommended dose.
Adjust the dose appropriately according to age and symptoms.

### ⚠️ 리오루펜정400밀리그람(이부프로펜) - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily and are taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.
Taking this medicine may increase the risk of serious and potentially fatal cardiovascular thrombotic reactions, myocardial infarction, and stroke, and may cause serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation.
Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg per day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes mellitus, smoking) who require high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg per day) should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine.
* Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to this medicine, gastrointestinal ulcers or bleeding (or history thereof), severe blood disorders, severe liver impairment, severe kidney impairment, severe cardiac dysfunction, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma (or history thereof), a history of hypersensitivity reactions such as asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other NSAIDs (including COX-2 inhibitors), pain before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, are in the last trimester of pregnancy, are undergoing high-dose methotrexate chemotherapy, or have genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.
Before taking this medicine, patients with a history of blood disorders, bleeding tendencies, liver cirrhosis, liver impairment (or history thereof), kidney impairment (or history thereof), cardiac dysfunction or heart disease, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus and mixed connective tissue disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, the elderly, children, pregnant women, or lactating women should consult a doctor or pharmacist.
