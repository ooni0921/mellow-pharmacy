### 💊 Hwaithuben Q Nose Soft Capsule - Indications & Effects
* This medication is used for the relief of various cold symptoms (runny nose, nasal congestion, sneezing, sore throat, cough, phlegm, chills (feeling cold and shivering), fever, headache, joint pain, muscle pain).

### 🕒 Hwaithuben Q Nose Soft Capsule - Dosage & Administration
* For individuals aged 15 years or older and adults, take 2 capsules per dose. For individuals aged 7 years or older but under 15 years, take 1 capsule per dose. Administer 3 times a day, 30 minutes after meals.

### ⚠️ Hwaithuben Q Nose Soft Capsule - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and plan to take this medication or other antipyretic analgesics, as it may cause liver damage.

Severe skin reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, and toxic epidermal necrolysis, which can very rarely be fatal, have been reported. If a skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reactions appear after taking this medication, discontinue use immediately.

Do not exceed the maximum daily dose of acetaminophen (4,000 mg), as it may cause liver damage. Do not take this medication with other products containing acetaminophen.

If severe skin adverse reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis occur, discontinue use and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
* Do not take this medication if you have a history of hypersensitivity to this drug, have experienced asthma when taking other antipyretic analgesics or cold medications, are an infant under 3 months of age, have severe or uncontrolled hypertension, or are in the 30th week of pregnancy or later.

Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are a child under 2 years of age; an infant or child under 15 years of age who is infected with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza; if symptoms of ischemic colitis (e.g., sudden abdominal pain, rectal bleeding) appear in relation to the pseudoephedrine component; if you have an allergic predisposition; have experienced allergic symptoms due to medication (e.g., fever, rash, joint pain, asthma, itching); have liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, hypertension, peptic ulcer, glaucoma (e.g., eye pain, blurred vision), or difficulty with urination; are elderly, frail, or have a high fever; have persistent or recurrent gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, stomach discomfort, or stomach pain, or have a history of ulcers or bleeding problems; are pregnant or suspect you may be pregnant; are breastfeeding; smoke; have asthma, chronic bronchitis, emphysema, a cough accompanied by excessive phlegm, a cough that lasts for more than a week or recurs, a chronic cough, or a cough accompanied by fever, rash, or persistent headache.

Adhere strictly to the prescribed dosage and administration.

When administering to children, do so under the guidance and supervision of a guardian.

Drowsiness may occur after taking this medication, so exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.

If symptoms do not improve after 5 to 6 doses, discontinue use immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
