### 💊 마이펜덱시연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Indications and Efficacy
*   This medicine is used for chronic polyarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis.
*   This medicine is used as an adjuvant therapy for painful swelling or inflammation after trauma and surgery, and for infections accompanied by inflammation, pain, and fever.

### 🕒 마이펜덱시연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
*   For adults, take 1 tablet at a time, 2 to 4 times a day.
*   Do not exceed 4 tablets per day.

### ⚠️ 마이펜덱시연질캡슐(덱시부프로펜) - Precautions
*   If you are a regular drinker who consumes three or more alcoholic drinks daily, consult a doctor or pharmacist when taking this medicine or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.
*   Taking this medicine may increase the risk of serious, potentially fatal, cardiovascular thrombotic events, myocardial infarction, and stroke. It may also cause severe gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation.
*   Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose dexibuprofen (1,200 mg/day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors such as hypertension, dyslipidemia, diabetes mellitus, or smoking, who require high-dose dexibuprofen, should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this medicine.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have hypersensitivity to this medicine, bronchial asthma, or a history of bronchial asthma, or a history of asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
*   Do not take this medicine if you have or show signs of gastrointestinal ulcers, a history of recurrence, gastrointestinal, cerebrovascular, or other site bleeding, inflammatory bowel diseases such as Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis, or a history of gastrointestinal bleeding or perforation due to previous NSAID use.
*   Do not take this medicine if you have severe hematological disorders, hepatic impairment, renal impairment, heart failure, hypertension, are undergoing pain treatment before or after coronary artery bypass graft surgery, or are pregnant in the third trimester (6 months of gestation or beyond).
*   Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have or have a history of hematological disorders, hepatic impairment, renal impairment, coagulation disorders, bleeding diathesis, fluid retention or heart failure, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) or mixed connective tissue disease (MCTD), gastric cancer, alcoholism, ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, cerebrovascular disease, hepatic porphyria, or cardiovascular disease risk factors such as hypertension, dyslipidemia, diabetes mellitus, smoking, or if you have undergone major surgery resulting in hypovolemia.
*   Before taking this medicine, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are pregnant in the first or second trimester, after 20 weeks of gestation, planning a pregnancy, possibly pregnant, elderly, a child over 6 years old, have a history of hypersensitivity reactions, or if you have gastrointestinal ulcers due to long-term NSAID administration in the past and require long-term administration of this medicine while also receiving treatment for peptic ulcers with misoprostol or similar drugs.
*   If you are a lactating woman, discontinue this medicine or stop breastfeeding.
*   Limit long-term administration to within 5 days.
*   Dizziness or fatigue may occur. Exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.
