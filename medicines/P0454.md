권장용량을 초과하여 복용하지 마십시오.

#EN
### 💊 이바펜400밀리그람정(이부프로펜) - Uses and Indications
* This medicine is used for rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis (degenerative joint disease), fever and pain due to common cold, lower back pain, dysmenorrhea (menstrual cramps), post-operative pain, ankylosing spondylitis, headache, toothache, muscle pain, neuralgia, acute gout, psoriatic arthritis, soft tissue injuries (sprains, contusions), and non-articular rheumatic conditions (tendinitis, tenosynovitis, bursitis).

### 🕒 이바펜400밀리그람정(이부프로펜) - Dosage and Administration
* For rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, soft tissue injuries, non-articular rheumatic conditions, acute gout, and psoriatic arthritis, adults should take 0.5 to 1.5 tablets (200-600 mg) per dose, 3-4 times daily, up to a maximum of 3200 mg per day.

For juvenile rheumatoid arthritis, take 30-40 mg per kg of body weight daily, divided into 3-4 doses. For mild to moderate pain or common cold, adults should take 0.5 to 1 tablet (200-400 mg) per dose, 3-4 times daily.

For children aged 11-14 years, take 200-250 mg per dose; for 7-10 years, take 150-200 mg per dose; for 3-6 years, take 100-150 mg per dose; for 1-2 years, take 50-100 mg per dose, 3-4 times daily, orally.

For children weighing less than 30 kg, the daily dose should not exceed 500 mg, and it is advisable to avoid taking on an empty stomach.

Do not exceed the recommended dose.

### ⚠️ 이바펜400밀리그람정(이부프로펜) - Precautions
* Regular drinkers of three or more alcoholic beverages daily should consult a doctor or pharmacist when taking this drug or other fever reducers/pain relievers. It may cause gastrointestinal bleeding.

Taking this drug may increase the risk of serious, potentially fatal cardiovascular thrombotic reactions, myocardial infarction, and stroke. Serious gastrointestinal adverse reactions such as gastrointestinal bleeding, ulceration, and perforation may also occur.

Patients with uncontrolled hypertension, congestive heart failure, established ischemic heart disease, peripheral arterial disease, or cerebrovascular disease should avoid high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg per day). Patients with cardiovascular risk factors (e.g., hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes mellitus, smoking) who require high-dose ibuprofen (2,400 mg per day) should consult a doctor or pharmacist before taking this drug.
* Do not take this drug if you have a hypersensitivity to it, gastrointestinal ulcers, gastrointestinal bleeding or a history thereof, severe blood disorders, severe liver dysfunction, severe kidney dysfunction, severe heart failure, severe hypertension, bronchial asthma or a history thereof, a history of hypersensitivity reactions such as asthma, urticaria, or allergic reactions to aspirin or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (including COX-2 inhibitors), pain occurring before or after coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery, are in the last trimester of pregnancy, are undergoing high-dose methotrexate chemotherapy, or have genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption.

Before taking this drug, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have blood disorders or a history thereof, a tendency to bleed, liver cirrhosis, liver dysfunction or a history thereof, kidney dysfunction or a history thereof, heart failure or heart disease, hypertension, systemic lupus erythematosus and mixed connective tissue disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, are elderly or a child, are pregnant, are breastfeeding, or have hypersensitivity to Yellow No. 5.
