#EN

### 💊 **한풍코프액** - Efficacy and Effects
*   This medication is used to relieve various cold symptoms (runny nose, nasal congestion, sneezing, sore throat, cough, phlegm, chills, fever, headache, joint pain, muscle pain).

### 🕒 **한풍코프액** - Dosage and Administration
*   Adults aged 15 years or older: Take 1 packet (90 mL) three times a day. Children aged 11 years to under 15 years: Take 2/3 packet (approx. 60 mL) three times a day. Children aged 7 years to under 11 years: Take 1/2 packet (approx. 45 mL) three times a day. Children aged 3 years to under 7 years: Take 1/3 packet (approx. 30 mL) three times a day. Children aged 2 years to under 3 years: Take 1/4 packet (approx. 22.5 mL) three times a day. Take 30 minutes after meals. If necessary, it can be taken at bedtime. In principle, the dosing interval should be 4 hours.

### ⚠️ **한풍코프액** - Precautions
*   Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics, as it may cause liver damage.

    Very rarely, serious skin reactions such as Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis (AGEP), Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS), and Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN), which can be fatal, have been reported. If skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reaction appear after taking this medication, discontinue use immediately.

    Do not exceed the maximum daily dose of acetaminophen (4,000 mg). It may cause liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.
*   Do not take this medication if you have hypersensitivity to it, its components, or if you have experienced asthma after taking other antipyretic analgesics or cold medicines. Infants under 3 months of age and children under 2 years of age should not take this medication.

    Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you are an infant or child under 15 years old with or suspected of having chickenpox or influenza, a child under 2 years old (only if absolutely necessary), a person with a predisposition to develop conditions such as urticaria, contact dermatitis, bronchial asthma, allergic rhinitis, migraine, or food allergies, a patient with a history of drug allergies, a person with liver disease, kidney disease, heart disease, thyroid disease, diabetes, hypertension, edema, gastroduodenal ulcer, glaucoma, or difficulty urinating, an elderly person, a frail person or a person with high fever, a person with persistent or recurrent gastrointestinal problems such as heartburn, stomach discomfort, or stomach pain, or those with ulcers or bleeding problems, pregnant women or women who may be pregnant, breastfeeding women, or a person with a cough accompanied by smoking, asthma, chronic bronchitis, emphysema, excessive phlegm, a cough lasting or recurring for more than one week, a chronic cough, or a cough accompanied by fever, rash, or persistent headache.

    Adhere strictly to the prescribed dosage and administration.

    Do not take continuously for a prolonged period.

    When administering to children, do so under the guidance and supervision of a guardian.

    Drowsiness may occur after taking this medication, so exercise caution when driving or operating machinery.

    If symptoms do not improve after 5-6 doses, discontinue use immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
