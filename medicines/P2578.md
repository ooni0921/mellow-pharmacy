#EN

### 💊 Go-Unzyme Mom Jung - Efficacy and Effects
*   This medication is used to supplement vitamins B1, B2, B6, B12, C, D, and E during pregnancy and lactation, physical fatigue, periods of decreased physical strength during or after illness, growth periods, and in old age. It is also used for the prevention of rickets and poor development of bones and teeth, and for the prevention and treatment of iron deficiency anemia.

### 🕒 Go-Unzyme Mom Jung - Dosage and Administration
*   For individuals 12 years and older, and adults, take 1 tablet once daily.

### ⚠️ Go-Unzyme Mom Jung - Precautions
*   Accidental overdose of iron-containing products is a leading cause of fatal poisoning in children under 6. Keep this product out of reach of children.
*   Do not take this medication if you have a hypersensitivity to any component of this medicine, hypercalcemia (excessive calcium in the blood), sarcoidosis (a systemic inflammatory disease of unknown cause), kidney disease, are an infant under 12 months, have Wilson's disease, hemochromatosis (a disorder of iron metabolism where iron deposits in the liver and pancreas), hemosiderosis, non-iron deficiency anemia, kidney stones, or severe renal failure.
*   Before taking this medication, consult a doctor or pharmacist if you have hyperoxaluria (excessive oxalate excretion in urine), are pregnant or may be pregnant, are breastfeeding, are a premature infant or infant, have gastrointestinal disorders such as peptic ulcer, chronic ulcerative colitis, or localized colitis, have heart or circulatory system dysfunction, kidney impairment, hypoproteinemia, gallbladder-related disease, liver disease, gout or kidney stones, a history of kidney stones, or folic acid deficiency.
*   Adhere strictly to the prescribed dosage and administration.
*   This medication may cause urine to turn yellow, affecting clinical test results or interfering with blood glucose detection during urine tests.
*   If symptoms do not improve after approximately one month of use, discontinue the medication immediately and consult a doctor or pharmacist.
