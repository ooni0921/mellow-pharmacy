#EN
### 💊 루트펜325밀리그램정(아세트아미노펜) - Indications & Effects
*   This medication is used for fever and pain caused by colds, headache, neuralgia, myalgia, dysmenorrhea (menstrual pain), sprain pain, toothache, arthralgia, and rheumatic pain.

### 🕒 루트펜325밀리그램정(아세트아미노펜) - Dosage & Administration
*   Adults should take 300-1,000 mg per dose, 3-4 times daily.

A maximum of 4,000 mg can be administered per day.

Dosage may be appropriately increased or decreased depending on age, condition, and symptoms.

Refer to the approved product labeling for detailed information.

### ⚠️ 루트펜325밀리그램정(아세트아미노펜) - Precautions
*   Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks per day and are taking this medication or other antipyretic analgesics. It may cause liver damage.

Severe skin reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, and toxic epidermal necrolysis, which can be fatal in very rare cases, have been reported. If a skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reaction appear after taking this medication, discontinue use immediately.

Do not exceed the maximum daily dose of acetaminophen (4,000 mg). It may cause liver damage. Do not take this medication with other products containing acetaminophen.
*   Patients with hypersensitivity to this medication, peptic ulcer, severe blood abnormalities, liver impairment, kidney impairment, decreased cardiac function, aspirin-induced asthma (asthma attacks induced by non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs) or a history thereof, those who have taken barbiturates, tricyclic antidepressants, or alcohol, and patients with genetic problems such as galactose intolerance, Lapp lactase deficiency, or glucose-galactose malabsorption should not take this medication.

Before taking this medication, patients with or with a history of liver impairment, kidney impairment, peptic ulcer, blood abnormalities, or bleeding tendencies, patients with cardiac dysfunction, bronchial asthma, elderly individuals, pregnant or lactating women should consult a doctor or pharmacist.
