### π’ μ λ…Ένμ •(μ•„μ„ΈνΈμ•„λ―Έλ…Έν) - Efficacy and Effects
* This drug is used for fever and pain caused by colds, headache, neuralgia, myalgia, menstrual pain, sprain pain, toothache, joint pain, and rheumatic pain.

### π•’ μ λ…Ένμ •(μ•„μ„ΈνΈμ•„λ―Έλ…Έν) - Dosage and Administration
* Children aged 12 years and older and adults should take 1-2 tablets at a time, 3-4 times a day (every 4-6 hours) as needed.

This drug should be taken at the lowest effective dose for the shortest possible duration, and the maximum daily dose should not exceed 4 g (8 tablets).

### β οΈ μ λ…Ένμ •(μ•„μ„ΈνΈμ•„λ―Έλ…Έν) - Precautions
* Consult a doctor or pharmacist if you regularly consume three or more alcoholic drinks daily and are taking this drug or other antipyretic analgesics, as it may cause liver damage.

Very rarely, severe skin reactions such as acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, and toxic epidermal necrolysis, which can be fatal, have been reported. If skin rash or other signs of hypersensitivity reactions appear after taking this drug, discontinue use immediately.

Do not exceed the maximum daily dose (4,000 mg) of acetaminophen. It can cause liver damage. Do not take with other products containing acetaminophen.
* Do not take this drug if you have hypersensitivity to this drug, peptic ulcer, severe blood disorders, liver dysfunction, kidney dysfunction, decreased heart function, aspirin asthma (asthma attacks induced by non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs), or a history of it, or if you are taking barbiturate drugs or tricyclic antidepressants, or if you have consumed alcohol.

Before taking this drug, patients with or with a history of liver dysfunction, kidney dysfunction, blood disorders, peptic ulcers, bleeding tendencies, impaired heart function, hypersensitivity, bronchial asthma, the elderly, pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a doctor or pharmacist.

Do not consume alcohol during treatment.
